# Copyright in the Netherlands

Copyright in the Netherlands (NL) is governed by the Dutch Copyright Act (DCA) known as [_Auteurswet_](https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0001886/2026-01-01). 

## Dutch Copyright Landscape 
Under the DCA, copyright protection automatically applies to original works of literature, science, or art that are perceptible to the senses (i.e., in fixed form). Protections last for the creator's life plus 70 years.

### International Treaties
The Netherlands is a signatory of: 
- [The Berne Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berne_Convention)
- [The Universal Copyright Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Copyright_Convention)
- [The WIPO Copyright Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WIPO_Copyright_Treaty)
- [The WIPO Performances and Phonograms Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WIPO_Performances_and_Phonograms_Treaty)
- [The Agreement on Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TRIPS_Agreement)

As a European Union (EU) member state, all EU copyright regulations, and treaties the EU is a signatory of, apply to NL, including the [Marrakesh Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marrakesh_VIP_Treaty), even though NL is not a signatory itself. 

### Summary
The copyright landscape in NL is [comparable to that of Canada](https://aeberuw.github.io/CCCertificateCourseWork/) with similar exceptions to copyright ([_Auteurswet_ Articles 15–25a](https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0001886/2026-01-01/?g=2026-01-01&z=2026-01-01#HoofdstukI_Paragraaf6)) as are seen [elsewhere](https://creativecommons.org/course/cc-cert-edu/unit-2-copyright-law/2-4-exceptions-and-limitations-to-copyright/). Interestingly, _Auteurswet_ copyright exceptions seem to be more permissive than those elsewhere, particularly for personal copies. However, in a [2014 court case](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/en/ALL/?uri=CELEX:62012CJ0435), the Court of Justice of the European Union ruled that creating [personal copies derived from unlawful sources is illegal](https://legalblogs.wolterskluwer.com/copyright-blog/no-more-downloading-from-unlawful-sources/), though [_Auteurswet_](https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0001886/2026-01-01/?g=2026-01-01&z=2026-01-01#HoofdstukI_Paragraaf6_Artikel16) and [copyright acts from other EU members](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40319-014-0295-7) do not expressly differentiate personal copies based on the source. Regardless, public norms and approaches to copyright in NL are influenced overall by their status as an EU member, a signatory of major international treaties, and [DCA amendments that support open access (OA)](https://www.openaccess.nl/en/policies/open-access-in-dutch-copyright-law-taverne-amendment).


## Creative Commons in the Netherlands
- The Netherlands have their own Creative Commons chapter, supported by the [Open Netherlands Association](https://www.opennederland.nl/), which is part of the international CC community.
- The Open Netherlands Association, a network of organizations (including Creative Commons NL) and professionals, promotes access to materials that can freely be used, edited, and shared.

The open movement is well-established and adopted throughout NL with the government implementing the [National Plan for Open Science](https://www.openscience.nl/en) (NPOS) which has been signed by organizations including funders, universities, and libraries. The NPOS supported the ambitious goal, [led by universities and libraries](https://web.archive.org/web/20180620191033/https://www.vsnu.nl/Roadmap-open-access-2018-2020-English/the-road-to-2020.html), to achieve 100% OA for publicly funded scientific publications by 2020. Since 2020, all publicly funded research publications must be OA and _reusable_.

### University Policies, cOAlition S, and Academic Norms
Most major funders in the Netherlands are part of [cOAlition S](https://www.coalition-s.org/), an international OA publishing initiative, which requires research to be OA with a CC license. As such, most [Dutch university OA policies](https://www.openaccess.nl/en/policies/institutional-policies) recommend using a CC BY license to meet [cOAlition S guidelines](https://www.coalition-s.org/plan_s_principles/) and the [Berlin Declaration](https://openaccess.mpg.de/Berlin-Declaration). With a strong mandate for open science from the government and organizations like Open Netherlands, Dutch academic and publishing traditions strongly support the use of CC licenses.

### Summary
Though 100% OA by 2020 wasn't achieved, 73% of articles from Dutch universities were [published OA in 2020](https://www.openaccess.nl/en/events/open-access-figures-for-2020-published-a-significant-increase-but-for-100-an-even-firmer), 11% more than 2019. Through a combination of a strong copyright landscape, government commitment to OA, [publishing agreements](https://www.openaccess.nl/en/about-open-access/history-of-open-access-in-the-netherlands), and an academic culture that values OA, Dutch researchers are well-positioned to continue and increase use of CC licenses.
